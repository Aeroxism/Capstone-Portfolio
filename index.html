Project Overview

The system aims to assist medical facilities by improving the management of patient records, personnel schedules, and operational workflows. It integrates AI-driven tools to support clinical decision-making, queue management, risk classification, and automated documentation.

Objectives of the Study

Main Objective

+

Develop a patient-centered web application for the Pediatrics Department of Tala Medical Hospital to organize patient data, enhance personnel scheduling, and improve care pathways through technology-driven approaches.

Specific Objectives

+
  • Evaluate existing processes and challenges in the Pediatrics Department.
  • Build a centralized and secure patient information module.
  • Implement AI-driven clinical decision support via recommendations.
  • Develop a queuing module for patient flow and bed assignment.
  • Create a risk classifier (low/moderate/high) for prioritization.
  • Provide interactive scheduling with visibility and confirmations.
  • Enable printing of certificates and slips.
  • Evaluate the system using TAM and ISO 25010 criteria.

Scope and Delimitation

Scope

  • Focus on Pediatrics Department of Tala Hospital (OPD, ER Triage).
  • Three access levels: Nurse, Doctor, Admin (if applicable).
  • AI for recommendation and chatbot modules.
  • Requires internet connectivity; desktop and tablet support.
  • Augments existing hospital scheduling; pediatrics-only features.

Delimitation

  • Not applied outside Pediatrics unless within admission flow.
  • Excludes financials: payments, amounts, billing.
  • Guidance accuracy depends on provided diagnostic/consultation data.
  • No formal diagnosis or prescriptions; not a replacement for clinicians.
  • Older devices may have performance limitations.
Note: Objectives and features may change based on validation and consultation with the Pediatrics Department to align with operational needs and feedback.
